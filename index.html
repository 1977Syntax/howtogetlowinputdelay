Tips to Reduce Input Delay

Use a LAN Cable

A wired connection can significantly reduce your input delay. Consider using a LAN cable instead of Wi-Fi.

Clear Your PC

Remove viruses and unnecessary files to improve performance. Use software like Malwarebytes and CCleaner.

Change DNS Settings

Use Google DNS (8.8.8.8) to potentially improve your connection speed.

Optimize Network Settings

Adjust your network card settings to maximize data throughput. Tools like TCP Optimizer can help.

Adjust MTU Settings

Set your Maximum Transmission Unit (MTU) to the optimal value for better performance.

Fortnite Settings Tips

Set Frame Rate Limit

Cap your FPS at your monitor's refresh rate to avoid unnecessary input delay.

Disable V-Sync

Turn off V-Sync in Fortnite settings to reduce input lag caused by frame syncing.

Lower Graphics Settings

Reduce your graphics settings to improve performance and minimize lag.

Enable Performance Mode

Switch to Performance Mode in Fortnite settings for better optimization.

Adjust Mouse Sensitivity

Fine-tune your mouse sensitivity settings for a smoother gameplay experience.

Key Facts

Using a wired connection can lower your ping by up to 50% compared to Wi-Fi.

Background applications can consume bandwidth and cause input delay.

Adjusting your MTU settings can lead to a smoother gaming experience.

Regularly clearing cache and temporary files can improve PC performance.

The distance between your gaming device and router can affect input delay.
