Practical Tips

  • Optimize Your Graphics Settings:

    Open Fortnite and go to the Settings menu. Navigate to the Graphics tab. Lower settings like shadows, anti-aliasing, and textures. Set your resolution to match your monitorâ€™s native resolution for optimal performance.

  • Use Wired Connections:

    Replace your wireless connection with an Ethernet cable. Connect one end to your router and the other to your gaming device. Wired connections minimize latency and provide a more stable internet connection.

  • Reduce Input Lag Through Monitor Settings:

    Access your monitor's menu and set the refresh rate to the highest available. Enable features like Game Mode or Low Input Lag Mode. Ensure V-Sync is turned off in both your monitor and game settings.

  • Disable Background Applications:

    Open Task Manager (Ctrl + Shift + Esc) and close unnecessary applications. Disable startup programs to improve performance when launching games.

  • Adjust Sensitivity Settings:

    Go to the game settings and find the Mouse Sensitivity options. Experiment with lower sensitivity settings for better control. Use a consistent DPI setting on your mouse for improved accuracy.

Five Key Facts

  1. Input delay can significantly affect gameplay.
  2. Refresh rate plays a crucial role in input responsiveness.
  3. Ping rates under 20ms are ideal for competitive gaming.
  4. Game settings can reduce overall latency.
  5. Hardware upgrades can improve input lag.
