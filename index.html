Five Essential Facts About Input Delay

1. Definition vs. Input Lag

Input delay refers to the time it takes for your actions to register in-game, whereas input lag refers to the delay between an input and its on-screen action.

2. Impact on Gameplay

Minimizing input delay improves reaction times and overall gameplay experience, allowing for more precise movements.

3. Advantages of Minimizing Input Delay

Statistics show that players with lower input delay have significantly better performance in competitive matches.

4. Common Misconceptions

Many believe input delay isn’t critical for casual gaming, but it can affect everyone, even in casual matches.

5. Professional Examples

Professional players often optimize their setups with specific hardware choices to ensure the lowest possible input delay.

Step-by-Step Guides

1. Adjusting In-Game Settings

a. Sensitivity Settings

Lower mouse sensitivity can lead to more precise aiming.

How to Adjust:

  1. Launch Fortnite and go to Settings.
  2. Navigate to the Input tab.
  3. Adjust the Mouse Sensitivity sliders.
  4. Use a consistent DPI setting on your mouse (400-800 DPI).

b. Frame Rate Limit

Set the frame rate limit to match your monitor’s refresh rate.

How to Adjust:

  1. In Settings, navigate to the Video tab.
  2. Find the Frame Rate Limit option.
  3. Set it to your monitor's refresh rate.

c. Resolution and Display Settings

A lower resolution can increase FPS.

How to Adjust:

  1. In the Video tab, locate the Resolution dropdown.
  2. Experiment with lower resolutions (e.g., 1600x900).
  3. Ensure Fullscreen mode is selected.

d. Graphics Quality

Reducing graphics quality can improve performance.

How to Adjust:

  1. Set Quality Presets to Low or Medium.
  2. Customize individual settings: Textures (Medium or Low), Shadows (Off), Anti-Aliasing (Off), V-Sync (Off), Motion Blur (Off).

Hardware Recommendations

Consider monitors with high refresh rates and low response times, along with gaming peripherals that are optimized for speed.
