Hardware Tips

1. Use an Ethernet Cable

Connecting your gaming device directly to the router using an Ethernet cable significantly reduces input delay and provides a more stable connection compared to Wi-Fi.

Benefit: Lower ping and reduced latency.

2. Upgrade Your Router

A high-quality router with advanced features can improve your connection stability and speed.

Benefit: Enhanced network performance for gaming.

3. Monitor Refresh Rate

Choose a monitor with a high refresh rate (144Hz or higher) for smoother gameplay.

Benefit: Better responsiveness in-game.

Network Optimization

4. Optimize Wi-Fi Settings

Ensure your router is positioned correctly, use the 5GHz band if available, and reduce interference from other devices.

Benefit: Improved signal strength and reduced latency.

5. Use Google DNS

Changing your DNS to Google's (8.8.8.8 and 8.8.4.4) can sometimes lower your ping.

Benefit: Potentially faster name resolution for servers.

Software Solutions

6. Clean Up Your PC

Use tools like Malwarebytes and Advanced SystemCare to remove unnecessary files and malware that can slow down your PC.

Benefit: Improved overall performance and FPS.

7. Use TCP Optimizer

This tool helps configure your internet settings for optimal performance. Remember to run it as an administrator.

Benefit: Unlocks the potential of your network card.
